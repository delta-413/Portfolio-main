About Me!

Interests

I first got interested in the computer science field when I was little and saw those movies where a hacker was typing away at light speed with green text scrolling through the screen. Of course that's not how coding works at all(although I wished it did lol).

Although I'm only knowledgeable in web pages, I still have lots of fun doing it! I participated in an extracurricular activity called TSA, Technology Student Association. It is a club that is about all things STEM. Most importantly, it allowed me to use my web design skills outside of school while I attended the club.

I also really like music, although I know next to nothing about it(I've only taken one class of music theory and still can't play any instruments) I still wanna learn more about it!

Slightly more related to coding, I've taken a video editing class and I enjoyed it! I loved collaberating with my friends when working on editing, it really made it so much funnier and memorable. I've learned to use Adobe Products like Photoshop and Premiere Pro in my video editing class.

Last but not least I'm REALLY into creating custom minecraft worlds using a program called WorldPainter. I absolutely love using WorldPainter, and I'm very eager to share what I've made with it! Expect more worlds to show up here because I'm not gonna stop making these!

Education

I have successfully completed a 3 year course of web design in high school. Before that, I've taken a Computer Science Pricipals class to get me started on my coding journey. My 3 year course of Web Design taught Html, Css, and Javascript!

I've also taken a video editing class and music theory class like I've mentioned, and my WorldPainter knowledge was mostly self taught after watching a few initial youtube tutorials.

Every Worldpainter project has this goal in mind: to learn how to use a feature I haven't used yet in that program. This makes every world I make fun and educational. The best part is seeing how far I've come by looking back on my first worlds compared to my most recent worlds.

Unlike the third world, this project was much more experimental. As always, I used a new feature on this world that I didn't use yet, and that was the ceiling dimension! I never made a cave as big as this, so it was a fun challenge! I struggled to think of what to put on the cave floor, as there was lots of empty spaces of stone. My solution was to flood most of the ground with lava. And from that I got to make outcroppings stick out from the lava, which I never made before, and only a small part of the terrain was above the lava apart from the stalagmites. As I type this, I just now get the idea that I could have put lots of cave crystals and other small features all over the empty spots to make them interesting without the need to flood anything. I'll keep this random idea in mind whenever I make another big cave!


There is a hole to the surface on the cave ceiling, but since I made the cave stretch from built limit to build limit, you can't do much on top of the cave like you would do on the nether roof.

The spot you spawn on is a gray sandy island that has lots of hot springs scattered on it. I didn't put any ground cover objects in this world, but that's oki.

I like how the walls came out, I wasn't liking how the walls were bare stone, and adding blobs of granite diorite and andesite wouldn't look good. I was given the idea to have layers like real life rock banding, and it really improved the cave walls!
